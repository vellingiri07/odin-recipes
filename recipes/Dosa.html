Dosa

Description

Dosa is the ever-popular South Indian breakfast of crispy crepes made with ground rice and lentil batter that is fermented. With just a handful of ingredients learn to make the classic Dosa recipe from scratch with my easy video and step-by-step photos.

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Vigna Mungo

Steps

  1. Rinse and soak rice (regular and parboiled) in water for 4-5 hours. Separately, rinse and soak urad dal, chana dal, and fenugreek seeds in water for the same duration.
  2. Drain the soaked urad dal mixture, reserving the water, and grind it until smooth and fluffy using the reserved water.
  3. Drain the soaked rice and grind it in batches to a smooth yet slightly grainy texture, using less water than for the dal.
  4. Mix the ground rice and dal batters in a large container, add salt, and stir well. Cover and ferment the batter overnight or for 8-10 hours at room temperature until it rises and bubbles.
  5. Heat a tawa or skillet on medium flame and grease it lightly with oil.
  6. Pour a ladle of batter onto the tawa, spread it in a spiral motion into a thin round, and drizzle oil or ghee around the edges. Cook until golden brown.
  7. Remove the dosa and repeat the process with the remaining batter. Serve hot with chutney or sambar.
